PROMPT_VERSION: aisidecar.prompt.whole_image/1.1.0

Return exactly one JSON object matching the supplied JSON Schema.

Do not wrap the response in Markdown.
Do not include code fences.
Do not include comments.
Do not add fields not present in the schema.
Use empty arrays when nothing is visible for an array field, except genre_or_photography_type, which must contain at least one best-fit entry.
Use an empty string for uncertainty_notes when there is no useful uncertainty note.

Use only visible image evidence.
Do not infer facts from what is merely likely.
Prefer a broader correct term over a narrower guess.
Do not use numeric confidence. Use only: high, medium, low.

Confidence bands:
- high: directly visible and unlikely to be wrong.
- medium: plausible from visible evidence, but not fully certain.
- low: weak visual support; include only when still useful for review.

Evidence strings must be short visual explanations, not reasoning traces.
Good evidence: "yellow bill and white body", "long legs in shallow water".
Bad evidence: "common in Florida", "probably because of the habitat".

Do not propose camera, lens, editing, software, file-format, color-profile, or image-quality keywords.
Use concise English terms suitable for later metadata normalization.

Analyze the complete photograph.

Emphasize the full frame: main subjects, secondary subjects, genre or photography type, scene context, habitat or setting, behavior or action, visible text, composition cues, lighting cues, and useful descriptive keywords.

Determine the likely genre or photography type of the image.
Use 1 to 4 items in genre_or_photography_type, ordered from most likely to less likely.

Choose only from this allowed vocabulary:
architecture, landscape, seascape, cityscape, portrait_people, group_portrait, candid_people, street_documentary, wildlife, bird_photography, plant_botanical, macro_closeup, abstract, still_life, product, food, sports_action, event, travel, night, astrophotography, aerial, underwater, interior, fine_art_conceptual.

Do not confuse subject matter with genre:
- a flower can be the subject while macro_closeup or plant_botanical is the genre
- a building can be the subject while architecture is the genre
- a person can be the subject while portrait_people, group_portrait, candid_people, or street_documentary is the genre
- a bird can be the subject while bird_photography is the genre
- a texture, reflection, or color field may support abstract even when object identification is weak

Use only visible evidence for the genre entry evidence string.
If two genres are both plausible, include both with appropriate confidence.
If no strong genre is clear, include the best broad match with low confidence.

GENRE-SPECIFIC GUIDANCE

Architecture:
- Look for buildings, interiors, structural elements, facades, columns, arches, windows, stairs, bridges, monuments, or designed built environments.
- Describe visible building type or function when obvious, such as church, office tower, house, museum, warehouse, bridge, or interior room.
- Useful keywords include visible architectural features, materials, symmetry, repetition, facade, arch, column, stairway, window, doorway, roofline, and structural detail.
- Do not infer named architectural style periods unless clearly supported by visible features.

Landscape:
- Use when broad natural scenery is the main subject.
- Look for landforms, sky, weather, water, vegetation, horizon, and scale.
- Useful descriptors include mountain, forest, desert, meadow, river, lake, cliff, marsh, wetland, dunes, open plain, and valley.
- If the sea, surf, shore, or coastal water dominates, seascape may be more appropriate than landscape.

Seascape:
- Use when ocean, sea, surf, coastline, beach, waves, boats on open water, or coastal atmosphere dominates the frame.
- Do not use seascape merely because a small amount of water is visible.

Cityscape:
- Use when the broader urban environment is a major subject, especially skylines, streetscapes, dense buildings, bridges, traffic, or night city views.
- Distinguish from architecture: architecture emphasizes one building or designed structure; cityscape emphasizes the urban scene as a whole.

Portrait / People:
- Use portrait_people when one person is the intentional visual focus.
- Use group_portrait when two or more people appear posed or presented as a group.
- Use candid_people when people are central but the image appears unposed or observed.
- Use street_documentary when people are part of an observational public-life scene.
- Describe visible pose, expression, clothing, role, gesture, and activity when useful.
- Do not infer identity, occupation, relationship, age, nationality, religion, or social role unless visible evidence clearly supports it.

Macro / Close-up:
- Use macro_closeup when the image strongly emphasizes small-scale detail, magnified appearance, or tight framing.
- Common subjects include insects, flowers, fungi, small objects, droplets, textures, mechanical details, jewelry, and surface patterns.
- Focus on texture, fine structure, color pattern, surface detail, and visible field marks.
- The subject may also belong to another genre such as wildlife, bird_photography, plant_botanical, product, food, or abstract.

Plant / Botanical:
- Use plant_botanical when plants, flowers, leaves, fungi, seeds, or botanical structures are the primary visual subject.
- If the image is an extreme close-up, include macro_closeup as an additional genre when supported.
- Do not infer exact species unless visible features support it.

Abstract:
- Use abstract when form, color, shape, line, repetition, reflection, blur, texture, or ambiguity dominates over literal subject identification.
- Do not force a literal subject if the image is primarily non-representational or visually ambiguous.
- Proposed keywords should favor visual qualities over weak object guesses.

Wildlife / Bird Photography:
- Use wildlife when a non-domesticated animal is central.
- Use bird_photography when birds are the clear focus.
- Identify animals only as specifically as visible evidence supports.
- Include visible behavior such as perched, flying, feeding, preening, swimming, running, resting, or interacting.
- Do not rely on habitat or expected range for species identification.

Still Life / Product / Food:
- Use still_life for arranged inanimate subjects.
- Use product for an object presented as the central subject, especially clean, isolated, catalog-like, or commercial-style presentation.
- Use food when prepared food, ingredients, drinks, table settings, or culinary presentation are the visual focus.
- Note arrangement, styling, containment, surface, label text, and presentation when visible.

Sports / Action:
- Use sports_action when athletic motion, competition, exercise, or sporting equipment is central.
- Note visible action such as running, jumping, tackling, swinging, throwing, racing, skating, or riding.
- Do not infer the exact sport if the visible evidence is weak.

Event / Travel / Documentary:
- Use event when a gathering, ceremony, performance, fair, contest, parade, or occasion is central.
- Use travel when depiction of a place, landmark, destination, or journey-oriented scene is central.
- Use street_documentary for observed public-life scenes, especially streets, sidewalks, transit, markets, crowds, or everyday urban activity.
- These may overlap; choose based on what the frame most strongly emphasizes.

Night / Astrophotography:
- Use night when nighttime or low-light presentation is central.
- Use astrophotography when stars, the Milky Way, constellations, moon, eclipse, aurora, meteor, or astronomical subject matter is central.
- Do not infer astrophotography from a dark sky alone when no celestial detail is visible.

Aerial / Underwater / Interior:
- Use aerial only when the viewpoint is clearly elevated overhead, drone-like, aircraft-like, or otherwise airborne.
- Use underwater only when the scene is visibly underwater.
- Use interior when an indoor built space is a major subject.

Fine Art / Conceptual:
- Use fine_art_conceptual when staged symbolism, conceptual construction, surreal arrangement, or art-object presentation dominates.
- Do not use it merely because the image is aesthetically composed.

WHOLE-IMAGE FIELD GUIDANCE

summary:
- One concise sentence about the full image.
- Include the main subject and the most important context.
- Do not mention uncertainty unless uncertainty is central.

main_subjects:
- List the dominant visible subjects.
- Use specific terms when supported, broader terms when uncertain.
- For wildlife, prefer broader accurate terms over weak species guesses.
- For people, describe visible person/persons without identity claims.

secondary_subjects:
- List meaningful visible secondary subjects, objects, animals, plants, structures, or scene elements.
- Exclude trivial background clutter unless useful for metadata.

scene_context:
- Describe the whole-frame situation: outdoor wildlife scene, studio portrait setup, street scene, interior scene, sporting event, product-style image, performance, or similar.
- Use only visible evidence.

habitat_or_setting:
- Describe visible environment or setting: wetland, beach, forest, garden, urban street, indoor room, studio, open water, sky, field, desert, snow, etc.
- Do not infer geographic location, season, or climate unless visible evidence directly supports it.

behavior_or_action:
- List visible behavior, pose, motion, interaction, or activity.
- Use plain visible terms such as flying, perched, walking, smiling, jumping, eating, swimming, standing, arranged, displayed, or resting.

visible_text:
- Include only readable or nearly readable text.
- Do not invent unreadable text.
- Use low confidence for partially readable text.

proposed_keywords:
- Provide concise metadata-oriented terms that could be useful later for XMP keyword normalization.
- Include subject terms, visible traits, behavior, setting, genre terms, and important visual attributes.
- Do not include duplicate terms already stated in exactly the same way unless the schema requires them.
- Do not include camera/lens/software/file-processing terms.
- Do not include private identity, sensitive attribute, or location guesses.

uncertainty_notes:
- Use this for uncertainty, partial occlusion, ambiguous species/object identity, weak text, or competing interpretations.
- Keep it brief.
- Use an empty string if there is nothing useful to note.

Return only the JSON object.
