GENRE-SPECIFIC GUIDANCE FOR ISOLATED SUBJECTS

Architecture:
- Use only if the isolated subject is clearly a building, architectural fragment, bridge, monument, interior structural element, window, arch, column, door, stairway, facade detail, or other built-environment subject.
- Do not infer cityscape from an isolated architectural fragment.
- Do not infer the building's use unless visible form or text supports it.

Landscape / Seascape / Cityscape:
- Use only when the isolated crop still contains a scene-like subject with strong environmental content.
- Most tight isolated crops should not use these genres because their context has been removed.
- Do not infer landscape, seascape, or cityscape from a subject that merely could have come from such a scene.

Portrait / People:
- Use portrait_people, group_portrait, or candid_people when the isolated subject is clearly a person or people.
- Focus on visible pose, expression, clothing, gesture, and interaction.
- Do not infer social context, event type, relationship, identity, occupation, religion, nationality, or age unless directly visible.
- Do not use street_documentary unless the isolated crop itself still preserves enough public-life context, which will usually be rare.

Macro / Close-up:
- Use macro_closeup when the crop emphasizes small-scale detail, magnified appearance, tight framing, or fine surface detail.
- This is often inferable from isolated subjects such as insects, flowers, tiny objects, textures, droplets, or detailed object fragments.
- Do not use macro_closeup merely because the subject is cropped; there must be a visible close-up or fine-detail emphasis.

Plant / Botanical:
- Use plant_botanical when the isolated subject is clearly a flower, leaf, seed, fruit on plant, fungus, stem, botanical structure, or plant detail.
- If species is uncertain, use broader terms such as flower, leaf, plant, fungus, or botanical detail.
- Do not infer garden, wildflower, habitat, or location.

Abstract:
- Use abstract when the isolated subject is dominated by shape, texture, pattern, blur, reflection, color, line, or ambiguity rather than clear literal depiction.
- Do not force object identification when the crop is visually ambiguous.
- Proposed keywords should favor visible visual qualities.

Wildlife / Bird Photography:
- Use wildlife when the isolated subject is clearly a non-domesticated animal.
- Use bird_photography when the isolated subject is clearly a bird.
- Focus on anatomy, plumage, markings, posture, bill shape, wing shape, tail shape, fur, scales, shell, or visible behavior.
- Do not use habitat, location, or expected range as evidence.
- If the crop shows only part of the animal, identify only as specifically as the visible parts support.

Still Life / Product / Food:
- Use still_life when the isolated subject is an arranged inanimate object or group of objects.
- Use product when the object is presented as a central item, especially with label, packaging, clean outline, commercial-style presentation, or isolated-object appearance.
- Use food when the subject is prepared food, ingredients, drink, dish, fruit, or culinary item.
- Do not infer brand unless readable text or visible logo clearly supports it.

Sports / Action / Event / Travel / Street Documentary:
- Be conservative. These often depend on scene context that may no longer be visible.
- Use sports_action only if the subject itself shows athletic action, sporting clothing, sporting equipment, or a clear sports pose.
- Use event only if the crop preserves clear event evidence such as costume, performance object, ceremonial object, stage element, or readable event text.
- Use travel or street_documentary only if the isolated crop itself preserves enough context, which will usually be rare.

Night / Astrophotography / Aerial / Underwater / Interior:
- Usually weak in isolated-subject mode unless directly visible in the subject itself.
- Use night only if the subject itself visibly contains low-light presentation, illumination, or nighttime cues.
- Use astrophotography only if astronomical subject matter remains visible.
- Use aerial only if the crop preserves an overhead or airborne perspective.
- Use underwater only if the subject itself shows underwater evidence.
- Use interior only if the isolated subject is itself an indoor structural or room element.

Fine Art / Conceptual:
- Use fine_art_conceptual only when the isolated subject visibly presents staged symbolism, conceptual construction, surreal arrangement, or art-object presentation.
- Do not use it merely because the subject is aesthetically isolated.
